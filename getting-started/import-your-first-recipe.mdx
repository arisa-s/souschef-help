One of the best things about Souschef is how quick it is to save a recipe the moment you find it. Whether you've just stumbled across a gorgeous pasta dish on Instagram, spotted a YouTube video you want to try, or you have a screenshot sitting in your camera roll — Souschef can pull that recipe in for you in seconds. Here's how to do it for the very first time.

## Import your first recipe

<Steps>
  <Step title="Download and open Souschef">
    If you haven't already, grab the free app from the [App Store](https://apps.apple.com/app/souschef-recipe-organizer/id6468939420) (iPhone/iPad) or [Google Play](https://play.google.com/store/apps/details?id=com.souschef.app) (Android). Once installed, open it up — you'll land on your recipe collection, which is empty and ready to fill!

    <Info>📸 Screenshot needed: the empty recipe collection screen on first launch</Info>
  </Step>
  <Step title="Tap the plus button">
    Look for the **+** button, usually found at the bottom of the screen. Give it a tap to start the import flow.

    <Info>📸 Screenshot needed: the main collection screen with the + button highlighted</Info>
  </Step>
  <Step title="Choose how you want to import">
    You've got a few options here — pick whichever suits the recipe you want to save:

    - **Paste a URL** — copy the link from a recipe website, Instagram, TikTok, YouTube, or Pinterest and paste it here.
    - **Share from another app** — if you're already browsing in Instagram, Safari, Chrome, or another app, use that app's Share button and select Souschef from the list.
    - **Scan a photo or screenshot** — tap the camera/image option to import a recipe from a photo in your camera roll or a screenshot you've taken.

    <Info>📸 Screenshot needed: the import options screen showing URL paste, share, and photo scan choices</Info>
  </Step>
  <Step title="Review and save the recipe">
    Souschef will process the recipe and show you a preview. Give it a quick look to make sure everything looks right — title, ingredients, and steps — then tap **Save**. That's it! Your recipe is now in your collection, ready to cook whenever you are. 🎉

    <Info>📸 Screenshot needed: the recipe preview screen before saving, with the Save button visible</Info>
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Tips for your first import

<Tip>
  **Start with a website recipe.** Recipe blogs and food websites tend to give the cleanest results. If you're testing Souschef for the first time, try pasting a link from your favourite food blog.
</Tip>

<Tip>
  **The Share button is your best friend.** Once you've [added Souschef to your share menu](/getting-started/add-to-share-menu), you can import any recipe without even opening the app first. Just tap Share in any app and select Souschef.
</Tip>

<Tip>
  **For video recipes, the link is all you need.** When importing from YouTube, TikTok, or Instagram, just copy and paste the URL for the video or post — Souschef will do the work of extracting the recipe for you.
</Tip>

## What to do if it doesn't work

Sometimes an import might not go quite as expected — a website might use an unusual layout, or a video might not have a full recipe in its description. Don't worry! Check out our guide on [why imports sometimes fail](/importing/why-import-fails) for common fixes and workarounds. You can also always type or paste a recipe in manually.
