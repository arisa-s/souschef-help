This feature is included in the free version of Souschef.

When a recipe step says something like "bake for 25 minutes" or "let the dough rest for 1 hour," Souschef highlights that time as a tappable timer. No more unlocking your phone, hunting for the clock app, and trying to remember what you just read — everything stays right where you're cooking.

## Starting a timer

Timers live right inside [Cooking Mode](/using/cooking-mode), so they're always within reach:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Look for highlighted time mentions">
    As you follow along in Cooking Mode, look for **highlighted time mentions** in the step text — these appear underlined with a timer icon.

    <img
      src="/images/screenshots/timers/timer-in-step.webp"
      alt="Cooking Mode step with bake for 1 hour highlighted as a tappable timer"
      className="help-screenshot"
    />
  </Step>
  <Step title="Tap the highlighted time">
    Tap the highlighted time to open the timer controls. Confirm the duration, then tap play to start the countdown.

    <img
      src="/images/screenshots/timers/timer-controls.webp"
      alt="Cooking Mode with a one-hour Bake timer ready to start and a play button"
      className="help-screenshot"
    />
  </Step>
  <Step title="Keep cooking">
    The timer runs directly in the app — you never have to leave your recipe to manage it.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Add a timer manually

You can also create a timer when a recipe doesn't include a time mention, or when you want a custom countdown:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Tap the timer button">
    In Cooking Mode, tap the timer button (clock with a plus) at the top of the screen.

    <img
      src="/images/screenshots/timers/add-timer-button.webp"
      alt="Cooking Mode with the add timer button highlighted"
      className="help-screenshot"
    />
  </Step>
  <Step title="Set the duration and start">
    Enter the time with the keypad (or tap **+ 30** to add 30 seconds), optionally name the timer, then tap **Start**.

    <img
      src="/images/screenshots/timers/timer-sheet.webp"
      alt="Untitled timer sheet with time display, numeric keypad, Reset, and Start"
      className="help-screenshot"
    />
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Managing multiple timers

Many recipes have overlapping steps, and Souschef handles that gracefully:

- You can run **several timers at the same time** — one for the pasta water, one for the sauce, one for the bread in the oven.
- All active timers stay visible without leaving Cooking Mode, so you can keep an eye on everything at a glance.
- If you open a [second recipe window](/using/cooking-mode#during-cooking), your timers continue running in the background.

<Tip>
  If a recipe has overlapping steps, start the longer timer first so both finish close together — it makes timing your meal much easier!
</Tip>
