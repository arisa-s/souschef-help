When a recipe step says something like "bake for 25 minutes" or "let the dough rest for 1 hour," Souschef highlights that time as a tappable timer. No more unlocking your phone, hunting for the clock app, and trying to remember what you just read — everything stays right where you're cooking.

## Starting a timer

Timers live right inside [Cooking Mode](/using/cooking-mode), so they're always within reach:

1. As you follow along in Cooking Mode, look for **highlighted time mentions** in the step text — these appear as tappable buttons.
2. Tap the highlighted time to start a countdown instantly.
3. The timer runs directly in the app — you never have to leave your recipe to manage it.

## Managing multiple timers

Many recipes have overlapping steps, and Souschef handles that gracefully:

- You can run **several timers at the same time** — one for the pasta water, one for the sauce, one for the bread in the oven.
- All active timers stay visible without leaving Cooking Mode, so you can keep an eye on everything at a glance.
- If you open a [second recipe window](/using/cooking-mode#during-cooking), your timers continue running in the background.

<Tip>If a recipe has overlapping steps, start the longer timer first so both finish close together — it makes timing your meal much easier!</Tip>
