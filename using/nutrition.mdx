Souschef shows nutrition information right inside your recipe so you can plan meals with a clear picture of what you're eating. When the source website already includes nutrition, Souschef uses that; when it doesn't, you can generate estimates with AI in a single tap. You can sort your library by calories per serving and edit any value yourself.

## What Souschef shows

Every recipe with nutrition data displays a per-serving breakdown of:

- **Calories**
- **Protein**
- **Carbohydrates**
- **Fat**
- **Additional nutrients** (fiber, sugar, sodium, and more when available)

## Where nutrition comes from

Souschef pulls nutrition data from two places:

- **From the source website**, when a recipe is imported from a site that publishes structured nutrition data.
- **From AI estimates**, when the source doesn't include nutrition or you've created a recipe yourself.

AI estimates are **clearly labeled** so you always know whether a number came from the original recipe or was estimated.

## Generate Nutrition with AI

If a recipe doesn't have nutrition information, generate an estimate in one tap.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the recipe">
    Tap any recipe in your library.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Tap 'Generate Nutrition'">
    In the nutrition section, tap **Generate Nutrition**. Souschef reads the ingredients, quantities, and servings and produces an estimate for calories, protein, carbs, fat, and additional nutrients.

    <img
      src="/images/screenshots/nutrition/generate-nutrition.webp"
      alt="Generate Nutrition button on a recipe with no nutrition data yet"
      className="help-ui-crop"
    />

  </Step>
  <Step title="Review the estimated values">
    The estimated nutrition is labeled so it's clear the values came from AI. Tap any number to edit it if you'd like a more precise value.

    <img
      src="/images/screenshots/nutrition/ai-generated-nutrition.webp"
      alt="Nutrition panel showing calories, protein, carbs, and fat labeled as AI Estimate"
      className="help-ui-crop"
    />

  </Step>
</Steps>

<Note>
  AI nutrition estimates are meant as a helpful guide, not medical or dietary
  advice. Actual values depend on brands, exact quantities, and how you cook —
  always double-check if you have specific dietary needs.
</Note>

## Edit nutrition manually

You can always override any value. Tap the nutrition section, then edit calories, protein, carbs, fat, or add more nutrients. Choose whether values are listed **Per serving** or **Per recipe**. This is handy when you know a value more precisely than the source or the AI does.

## Sort your library by calories

Looking for something lighter tonight? Open the sort menu on your library and choose **Calories per serving (high)** or **Calories per serving (low)**.

<img
  src="/images/screenshots/nutrition/sort-by-calories.webp"
  alt="Sort menu with Calories per serving high and low options"
  className="help-screenshot"
/>

<Tip>
  Nutrition information becomes more useful the more accurate your servings are.
  Before generating nutrition, double-check the recipe's servings so per-serving
  values are meaningful.
</Tip>
