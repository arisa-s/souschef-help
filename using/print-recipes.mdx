This feature is included in the free version of Souschef.

Sometimes paper just works better. Whether you like keeping a printed copy in a family cookbook, cooking without a phone in the kitchen, or handing a recipe to someone who prefers it on paper, Souschef makes it easy to print any recipe from your library in a clean, kitchen-ready format.

## What a printed recipe includes

When you print a recipe, Souschef uses:

- Your **current serving size** — whatever you have the recipe scaled to when you print
- Your **measurement preference** — the units currently shown in the app
- The recipe **title**
- The recipe **photo**
- **Prep time**
- **Cook time**
- **Ingredients**
- **Numbered step-by-step instructions**
- A **QR code** that links back to the recipe in Souschef
- A **printable checklist** you can tick as you shop or cook

## How to print a recipe

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the recipe">
    Tap the recipe in your library.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Adjust the serving size and units (optional)">
    Change the [serving size](/using/adjust-servings) or [measurement units](/using/convert-measurements) before printing — the print output uses whatever's shown on screen.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Open the recipe menu">
    Tap the **⋯** button in the top right of the recipe.

    <img
      src="/images/screenshots/print/more-button.webp"
      alt="Recipe screen with the more options button highlighted in the top right"
      className="help-screenshot"
    />
  </Step>
  <Step title="Tap Print recipe">
    Choose **Print recipe** from the menu.

    <img
      src="/images/screenshots/print/print-recipe-option.webp"
      alt="Recipe menu with Print recipe highlighted"
      className="help-screenshot"
    />
  </Step>
  <Step title="Send it to your printer">
    Use your device's standard print sheet to send the recipe to an AirPrint or Google Cloud Print–compatible printer, or save the recipe as a PDF to keep or share.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Tip>
  Save a printed recipe as a PDF and drop it into a shared folder to build a family cookbook that everyone can access.
</Tip>

## The QR code

Every printed recipe includes a QR code at the bottom. Scan it with any phone camera to open the live recipe link. If you have the app installed, you can jump into [Cooking Mode](/using/cooking-mode) for timers and step-by-step guidance. If someone doesn't have the app, the same link opens in Souschef's lightweight web viewer so they can still read the recipe, adjust servings, and switch measurement systems.
