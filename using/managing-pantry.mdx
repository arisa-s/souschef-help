Once your [Pantry](/using/pantry) has some items in it, day-to-day upkeep is quick. Edit an item's location or category, adjust its quantity as you cook, update expiration dates, or remove things you've used up — all in a couple of taps.

## Edit an item

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open Pantry">
    Tap the **Pantry** tab in Souschef.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Tap the item you want to edit">
    Find the ingredient in the list — use search or filter by location if you have a lot of items.

    <Info>
      📸 Screenshot needed: Pantry list view with the search bar highlighted and an item ready to tap.
    </Info>
  </Step>
  <Step title="Update the details">
    Change the name, location (Fridge/Freezer/Pantry), category, quantity, or expiration date. Tap **Save** to keep your changes.

    <Info>
      📸 Screenshot needed: the edit sheet for a Pantry item with location, category, quantity, and expiration date fields visible.
    </Info>
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Move items between locations

Moved the cheese from the counter into the fridge? Just open the item and change its location. All Pantry views and the **In Pantry** badge on shopping lists update immediately.

## Remove items

When you've used up an ingredient, remove it in one of two ways:

- **Swipe to delete** from the Pantry list, or
- Open the item and tap the **Delete** option.

<Tip>
  If you commonly re-buy an item (like milk or eggs), consider keeping it in your Pantry with just its quantity updated — that way it always stays on your radar and continues skipping your shopping list when you have enough.
</Tip>

## Search and filter

Got a big Pantry? Use the search bar at the top to find any item by name. You can also filter by location (Fridge, Freezer, Pantry) or by category (produce, dairy, spices, and so on).

## Keeping your Pantry accurate

A Pantry is only as useful as it is up to date. Two habits keep it accurate with almost no effort:

1. **Scan your receipt after every grocery trip** — see [Receipt Scanning](/using/receipt-scanning).
2. **Remove or reduce quantities as you cook** — doing this while you're already in the kitchen takes a second and pays off the next time you plan a meal.
