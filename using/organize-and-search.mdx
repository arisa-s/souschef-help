All your imported recipes are stored right inside Souschef and available offline — no internet connection needed once a recipe is saved. Whether you've imported a handful of family favorites or built up a library of hundreds of dishes, Souschef's search and filters make finding exactly what you're looking for a breeze. Filter by AI-suggested [tags](/using/ai-tagging) like cuisine, meal type, diet, main ingredient, or cooking method — or by [calories per serving](/using/nutrition) when you're planning something lighter.

## Searching for a recipe

Finding a recipe takes just a few taps:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the search bar">
    Open Souschef and tap the **search bar** at the top of your recipe list.

    <Info>
      📸 Screenshot needed: Home screen with the search bar highlighted at the top of the recipe list.
    </Info>
  </Step>
  <Step title="Type a keyword">
    Type a dish name, a main ingredient, or anything you remember about the recipe.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Find your recipe">
    Results appear as you type, so you'll usually spot what you're looking for before you even finish typing.

    <Info>
      📸 Screenshot needed: Search results updating in real time as the user types a keyword.
    </Info>
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Tip>
  Give your recipes descriptive names when saving so they're easy to find later. For example, "Mom's Chicken Tikka Masala" is much easier to search for than just "Chicken."
</Tip>

## Filter by tag

Every imported recipe gets [AI-suggested tags](/using/ai-tagging) — cuisine, meal type, diet, main ingredient, cooking method, and more. Use the filter drawer above your library to narrow the list to just the recipes that match.

Common combinations:

- **Vegetarian \+ Dinner** — an instant weeknight shortlist.
- **Italian \+ Pasta** — everything you have saved from that Sunday sauce era.
- **Quick \+ Kid-friendly** — realistic weeknight ideas.

## Filter by calories

Cooking lighter tonight? Use the **calories per serving** filter to show only recipes below a certain number. Nutrition data comes from source websites when available, and can be [generated with AI](/using/nutrition) for any recipe that's missing it.
