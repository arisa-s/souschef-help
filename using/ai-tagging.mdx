Good news — AI Tagging is available to everyone using Souschef.

Every time you import a recipe, Souschef's AI reads the recipe and suggests a set of tags that describe it — cuisine, meal type, diet, main ingredient, cooking method, and more. Suggested tags are pre-selected but always editable, and they power the filters throughout your library.

## What Souschef tags

Suggested tags cover the ways you actually think about recipes:

- **Cuisine** — Italian, Japanese, Mexican, Thai, and so on
- **Meal type** — breakfast, lunch, dinner, snack, dessert
- **Diet** — vegetarian, vegan, gluten-free, high-protein, and more
- **Main ingredient** — chicken, pasta, tofu, salmon, and so on
- **Cooking method** — baked, grilled, air fryer, one-pot, slow cooker, no-cook
- **More** — quick, kid-friendly, meal prep, holiday, and other contextual tags

## Tags during import

When you import a recipe from any source — [Instagram](/importing/import-from-instagram), [TikTok](/importing/import-from-tiktok), [YouTube](/importing/import-from-youtube), [Pinterest](/importing/import-from-pinterest), [Facebook](/importing/import-from-facebook), a [website](/importing/import-from-website), [photos](/importing/import-from-photos), or [plain text](/importing/import-from-plain-text) — the import preview shows Souschef's suggested tags with the most relevant ones already selected.

You can:

- **Accept the suggestions** as-is with a single tap on Save.
- **Untick tags** that don't feel right.
- **Tap to add** additional tags Souschef didn't suggest.

## Editing tags on a saved recipe

Already saved a recipe? You can still edit its tags anytime.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the recipe and tap the tags">
    Open the recipe from your library and tap the tag row under the recipe details.

    <img
      src="/images/screenshots/ai-tagging/recipe-tags.webp"
      alt="Recipe detail with the tags row highlighted showing blueberry, dessert, and lemon"
      className="help-screenshot"
    />

  </Step>
  <Step title="Add or remove tags">
    Type a new tag in **Add a tag**, or tap the **x** on a selected tag to remove it. Tap **Done** when you're finished. Changes apply immediately in your library filters.

    <img
      src="/images/screenshots/ai-tagging/tag-editor-sheet.webp"
      alt="Tags sheet with selected blueberry, dessert, and lemon tags"
      className="help-screenshot"
    />

  </Step>
</Steps>

## Managing tags in your library

Rename tags, move them into the right category for library filters, or add new ones from Account settings.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open Account settings">
    On the Saved tab, tap your **profile** icon in the top right.

    <img
      src="/images/screenshots/ai-tagging/account-button.webp"
      alt="Saved recipes screen with the account profile icon in the top right"
      className="help-screenshot"
    />

  </Step>
  <Step title="Tap Manage tags">
    Under **Kitchen**, tap **Manage tags**.

    <img
      src="/images/screenshots/ai-tagging/manage-tags-link.webp"
      alt="Account settings with Manage tags highlighted under Kitchen"
      className="help-screenshot"
    />

  </Step>
  <Step title="Review your tags">
    Tags are grouped by category, such as Cuisine and Meal type. Tap **+ Add tag** to create a new one, or tap an existing tag to edit it.

    <img
      src="/images/screenshots/ai-tagging/manage-tags-screen.webp"
      alt="Recipe tags screen listing Cuisine and Meal type tags"
      className="help-screenshot"
    />

  </Step>
  <Step title="Rename, recategorize, or delete">
    Change the tag name, pick a different category for library filters, or delete the tag. Then tap **Save**.

    <img
      src="/images/screenshots/ai-tagging/edit-tag-sheet.webp"
      alt="Edit tag sheet with name and category fields"
      className="help-screenshot"
    />

  </Step>
</Steps>

## Filtering your library by tag

Tags become powerful the moment you have more than a handful of recipes. In your library:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Tap the filter icon">
    Open the filters drawer above your recipe list.

    <img
      src="/images/screenshots/organize/filter-button.webp"
      alt="Saved recipes screen with the filter button highlighted next to the search bar"
      className="help-screenshot"
    />

  </Step>
  <Step title="Pick one or more tags">
    Filter by any combination of cuisine, meal type, diet, main ingredient, cooking method, or more.

    <img
      src="/images/screenshots/organize/filter-sheet.webp"
      alt="Filter drawer showing tag categories for narrowing the recipe library"
      className="help-screenshot"
    />

  </Step>
  <Step title="See only matching recipes">
    Your library instantly narrows to recipes that match all selected tags.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Tip>
  Combine a diet tag with a meal type — like **vegetarian** \+ **dinner** — to pull up an instant weeknight shortlist.
</Tip>
