Importing from TikTok is included in the free version of Souschef.

Got a TikTok recipe you've been meaning to try? With Souschef, you can save it and have a neatly organized recipe ready to cook — no more digging through your TikTok saved videos or re-watching a video just to remember the ingredients. Souschef extracts recipe information from the post's caption and, when available, the video's spoken audio. There are two ways to do it, so pick whichever works best for you!

<Note>
  Souschef works best when the recipe is included in the post caption or spoken aloud in the video. If a post only shows the finished dish without explaining the recipe, Souschef may not have enough information to create a complete recipe.
</Note>

## How to import a recipe from TikTok

<Tabs>
  <Tab title="Method 1: Share from TikTok">
    Already watching a recipe in the TikTok app? Share it straight to Souschef.

    <Steps>
      <Step title="Find a recipe and tap the ··· (Send to) button">
        Find a TikTok recipe post and tap the **···** (**Send to**) button.

        <img
          src="/images/screenshots/tiktok/step1.gif"
          alt="TikTok recipe post with the Send to button highlighted"
          className="help-screenshot"
        />
      </Step>
      <Step title="Swipe through the social icons">
        Swipe through the row of social icons in the share menu.

        <img
          src="/images/screenshots/tiktok/step2.gif"
          alt="TikTok share menu showing social icons to swipe through"
          className="help-screenshot"
        />
      </Step>
      <Step title="Tap the ··· (More) button">
        Tap the **···** (**More**) button to open the full list of apps.

        <img
          src="/images/screenshots/tiktok/step3.gif"
          alt="TikTok share menu with the More button highlighted"
          className="help-screenshot"
        />
      </Step>
      <Step title="Select the Souschef icon">
        Tap the **Souschef** app icon. Souschef will open and start extracting the recipe from the post's caption and spoken audio when available.

        <img
          src="/images/screenshots/tiktok/step4.gif"
          alt="Share sheet with the Souschef app icon highlighted"
          className="help-screenshot"
        />

        Don't see Souschef? Follow [Add Souschef to your share menu](/getting-started/add-to-share-menu).
      </Step>
    </Steps>
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Method 2: Paste a URL">
    Already have a TikTok link you want to save? You can paste the URL directly into Souschef.

    <Steps>
      <Step title="Copy the TikTok URL">
        In the TikTok app or in your browser, open the recipe post and copy its URL.
      </Step>
      <Step title="Open Souschef and tap '+'">
        Open Souschef and tap the **\+** button at the bottom of your collection.
      </Step>
      <Step title="Tap Import from URL">
        Select **Import from URL** from the options.
      </Step>
      <Step title="Paste the URL and tap Import">
        Paste the TikTok URL and tap **Import**. Souschef will extract the recipe and add it to your collection.
      </Step>
    </Steps>
  </Tab>
</Tabs>

<Tip>
  Stay in the app while Souschef processes the recipe — don't close it. The import can take up to 30 seconds, and then your recipe will be ready and waiting for you.
</Tip>
