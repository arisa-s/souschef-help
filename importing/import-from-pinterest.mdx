Pinterest is a wonderful place to discover recipe ideas, but keeping track of all those pins can quickly turn into a mess. With Souschef, you can import any Pinterest recipe directly into your collection — where you can scale servings, build a shopping list, and cook without the clutter. There are two ways to do it, so pick whichever works best for you!

<Tabs>
  <Tab title="Method 1: In-app Explorer">
    Souschef has a built-in Pinterest explorer that lets you browse and import recipes without ever leaving the app.

    <Steps>
      <Step title="Tap the '+' button in Souschef">
        Open Souschef and tap the **"+"** button to add a new recipe.
        <Info>📸 Screenshot needed: Souschef home screen with the + button highlighted</Info>
      </Step>
      <Step title="Tap the Pinterest logo">
        In the new recipe screen, tap the **Pinterest logo** to open the in-app Pinterest explorer.
        <Info>📸 Screenshot needed: Souschef new recipe screen showing the Pinterest logo option</Info>
      </Step>
      <Step title="Browse and select a recipe">
        Browse through Pinterest recipes and tap the one you want to import. Souschef will extract the recipe details automatically and bring them into your collection.
        <Info>📸 Screenshot needed: Souschef in-app Pinterest explorer with a recipe selected</Info>
      </Step>
    </Steps>
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Method 2: Paste a URL">
    Already have a Pinterest pin you want to save? You can paste the link directly into Souschef.

    <Steps>
      <Step title="Copy the pin URL">
        In the Pinterest app or in your browser, open the pin and copy its URL.
      </Step>
      <Step title="Open Souschef and tap '+'">
        Open Souschef and tap the **"+"** button to add a new recipe.
        <Info>📸 Screenshot needed: Souschef home screen with the + button highlighted</Info>
      </Step>
      <Step title="Tap 'Import from URL'">
        Select **"Import from URL"** from the options.
      </Step>
      <Step title="Paste the URL and tap Import">
        Paste the Pinterest pin URL into the field and tap **Import**. Souschef will extract the recipe in just a moment.
        <Info>📸 Screenshot needed: Souschef Import from URL screen with a Pinterest URL pasted in</Info>
      </Step>
    </Steps>
  </Tab>
</Tabs>
