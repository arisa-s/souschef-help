Souschef can pull recipes from almost anywhere you find food inspiration — whether you're scrolling through social media, watching a cooking video, browsing a recipe blog, flipping through a printed cookbook, or copy-pasting a family favorite from a text message. In most cases, it's just a tap or two away. Here's every source Souschef currently supports, with links to detailed guides for each one.

## AI Discovery: search across sources at once

Don't have a specific link? Use [AI Discovery](/using/ai-discovery) to describe what you're craving and Souschef will search across recipe websites, Instagram, TikTok, Facebook, and YouTube in one go — then let you import any result directly into your collection.

## How importing works

Most sources work through your phone's **share sheet** — the menu that appears when you tap the Share button inside any app. Tap Share, find the Souschef icon, and the import kicks off automatically. If you don't see Souschef in your share sheet, check out [how to add Souschef to your share menu](/getting-started/add-to-share-menu).

For sources without a share sheet flow — or when you just have text — use the **\+** button inside Souschef to paste a URL, paste recipe text, or create a recipe manually.

<Note>
  Every imported recipe gets [AI-suggested tags](/using/ai-tagging) (cuisine, meal type, diet, main ingredient, cooking method, and more) automatically. Nutrition data comes through when the source provides it, and you can [generate nutrition estimates](/using/nutrition) with AI when it doesn't.
</Note>
