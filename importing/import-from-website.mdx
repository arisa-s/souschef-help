Found a great recipe on a blog or recipe website? You don't need to manually type it all out — just paste the URL into Souschef and it will automatically extract the ingredients, instructions, and other details for you. Souschef works with most popular recipe websites, so getting set up takes only a few seconds.

## How to import a recipe from a website

<Steps>
  <Step title="Copy the recipe URL">
    Find a recipe on any blog or recipe website and copy the page's URL from your browser's address bar.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Open Souschef and tap '+'">
  </Step>
  <Step title="Tap 'Import from URL' and paste the link">
  </Step>
  <Step title="Tap Import and wait a moment">
    Tap **Import**. Souschef will fetch the page and extract the recipe — this usually only takes a few seconds.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Review the recipe and tap Save">
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Alternative: Use your browser's share sheet

If you're already on the recipe page in your mobile browser, you can skip the copy-paste step:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Tap the Share button in your browser">
  </Step>
  <Step title="Select Souschef from the share sheet">
    Choose **Souschef** from the share sheet options. Souschef will open and start importing the recipe automatically. If you don't see Souschef in the share sheet, follow the guide to [add Souschef to your share menu](/getting-started/add-to-share-menu).
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Tip>
  Most popular recipe blogs work great with Souschef. If a site uses a non-standard layout or custom format, the import might be incomplete — but you can edit any field before saving, so it's easy to fill in anything that's missing.
</Tip>

## Troubleshooting

If the import didn't work as expected, see [Why a recipe import might fail](/importing/why-import-fails) for common causes and how to fix them.
