Importing from websites is included in the free version of Souschef.

Found a great recipe on a blog or recipe website? You don't need to manually type it all out — share the page to Souschef (or paste the URL) and it will automatically extract the ingredients, instructions, and other details for you. Souschef works with most popular recipe websites, so getting set up takes only a few seconds.

## How to import a recipe from a website

There are two ways to import a website recipe. Pick whichever works best for you.

<Tabs>
  <Tab title="Method 1: Share from your browser">
    Already on the recipe page in your mobile browser? Share it straight to Souschef.

    <Tabs>
      <Tab title="iPhone (iOS)">
        <Steps>
          <Step title="Open the recipe and tap Share">
            Open Safari (or any browser), go to the recipe page, and tap the **Share** button — it looks like a box with an arrow pointing upward, and you'll usually find it at the bottom of the screen.

            <img
              src="/images/screenshots/share-menu/ios-step1.webp"
              alt="Safari address bar with the Share button highlighted"
              className="help-ui-crop"
            />
          </Step>
          <Step title="Tap the Souschef icon">
            Choose **Souschef** from the share sheet. Souschef will open and start importing the recipe automatically.

            <img
              src="/images/screenshots/share-menu/ios-step2.webp"
              alt="iOS share sheet with app icons"
              className="help-ui-crop"
            />

            Don't see Souschef in the share sheet? Follow [Add Souschef to your share menu](/getting-started/add-to-share-menu).
          </Step>
          <Step title="Review the recipe and tap Save">
            Souschef will fetch the page and extract the recipe — this usually only takes a few seconds. Review the details, edit anything that needs tweaking, and tap **Save**.
          </Step>
        </Steps>
      </Tab>
      <Tab title="Android">
        <Steps>
          <Step title="Open the recipe and tap Share">
            Open Chrome (or any browser), go to the recipe page, and tap the **Share** button — you'll usually find it in the browser's menu or toolbar.

            <img
              src="/images/screenshots/share-menu/android-step1.webp"
              alt="Android browser toolbar with the Share button highlighted"
              className="help-ui-crop"
            />
          </Step>
          <Step title="Tap the Souschef icon">
            Tap the **Souschef** icon in the share sheet. Souschef will open and start importing the recipe automatically.

            <img
              src="/images/screenshots/share-menu/android-step2.webp"
              alt="Android share sheet with Souschef highlighted in the app list"
              className="help-ui-crop"
            />

            Don't see Souschef in the share sheet? Follow [Add Souschef to your share menu](/getting-started/add-to-share-menu).
          </Step>
          <Step title="Review the recipe and tap Save">
            Souschef will fetch the page and extract the recipe — this usually only takes a few seconds. Review the details, edit anything that needs tweaking, and tap **Save**.
          </Step>
        </Steps>
      </Tab>
    </Tabs>
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Method 2: Paste a URL">
    Already have a recipe link you want to save? You can paste the URL directly into Souschef.

    <Steps>
      <Step title="Copy the recipe URL">
        Find a recipe on any blog or recipe website and copy the page's URL from your browser's address bar.
      </Step>
      <Step title="Open Souschef and tap '+'">
        Open Souschef and tap the **\+** button at the bottom of your collection.
      </Step>
      <Step title="Tap Import from URL">
        Select **Import from URL** from the options.
      </Step>
      <Step title="Paste the URL and tap Import">
        Paste the recipe URL and tap **Import**. Souschef will fetch the page and extract the recipe — this usually only takes a few seconds.
      </Step>
      <Step title="Review the recipe and tap Save">
        Review the details, edit anything that needs tweaking, and tap **Save**.
      </Step>
    </Steps>
  </Tab>
</Tabs>

<Tip>
  Most popular recipe blogs work great with Souschef. If a site uses a non-standard layout or custom format, the import might be incomplete — but you can edit any field before saving, so it's easy to fill in anything that's missing.
</Tip>
