Importing from photos is included in the free version of Souschef.

Got a recipe saved as a screenshot, a photo of a cookbook, or even a printed recipe card on the fridge? Souschef's Scan feature lets you digitize any recipe quickly and easily — just take a photo or choose one from your library, and Souschef will extract the ingredients and instructions for you. No more hunting through camera rolls or squinting at printed pages!

## How to scan and import a recipe

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open Souschef, tap '+', then select Scan">
    Open the Souschef app, tap the **\+** button, and select **Scan**.

    <img
      src="/images/screenshots/photos/step1.gif"
      alt="Souschef import screen with the Scan option highlighted"
      className="help-screenshot"
    />
  </Step>
  <Step title="Take a photo or choose from your library">
    Take a new photo or select existing ones from your library. Make sure the ingredients and instructions are clearly visible.

    <img
      src="/images/screenshots/photos/step2.webp"
      alt="Photo picker showing recipe images ready to scan"
      className="help-screenshot"
    />

    <Note>
      If your recipe spans more than one page — like a cookbook spread or a long printed recipe — you can upload up to 3 photos at once.
    </Note>
  </Step>
  <Step title="Crop the photo to one recipe">
    Crop the photo so it includes only one recipe, especially if your image contains more than one.

    <img
      src="/images/screenshots/photos/step3.webp"
      alt="Crop screen focusing on a single recipe"
      className="help-screenshot"
    />

    <Tip>
      Cropping to only the relevant recipe helps Souschef extract ingredients and instructions more accurately. Make sure the cropped photo shows just one recipe — not multiple recipes or unrelated text.
    </Tip>
  </Step>
  <Step title="Tap Proceed to scan the recipe">
    Tap **Proceed** and Souschef will automatically scan and extract the recipe in just a few seconds.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Review, edit, and save">
    Review the extracted details, make any quick edits, and tap **Save**. Your recipe is now searchable, shareable, and ready to cook anytime.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Tip>
  Make sure the ingredients and instructions are clearly visible in the photo. Good lighting makes a big difference — try taking the photo in a well-lit area, or increase your screen brightness if you're scanning a screenshot.
</Tip>

## Troubleshooting

If the scan came back with missing or incorrect details, you can always edit any field inside Souschef before saving. For more common issues, see [Why a recipe import might fail](/importing/why-import-fails).
