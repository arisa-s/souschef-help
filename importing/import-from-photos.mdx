Got a recipe saved as a screenshot, a photo of a cookbook, or even a printed recipe card on the fridge? Souschef's Scan feature lets you digitize any recipe quickly and easily — just take a photo or choose one from your library, and Souschef will extract the ingredients and instructions for you. No more hunting through camera rolls or squinting at printed pages!

## Steps to import

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open Souschef, tap '+', then select 'Scan'">
    Open the Souschef app and tap the **"+"** button to add a new recipe. From the options that appear, select **"Scan"**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Take a photo or choose from your library">
    You can take a new photo on the spot, or select existing photos from your library. If your recipe spans multiple pages, you can select **up to 3 photos** at once to cover the whole thing.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Crop the photo to one recipe">
    Use the crop tool to frame the image so it includes only one recipe. If your photo has multiple recipes or a lot of extra content around the edges, cropping it tightly helps Souschef focus on what matters.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Tap Proceed to scan the recipe">
    Tap **Proceed** and Souschef will automatically scan and extract the recipe in just a few seconds.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Review, edit, and save">
    Look over the extracted details — title, ingredients, steps, and any notes. Make any edits you need, then tap **Save**. Your recipe is now searchable and ready to cook from, anytime!
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Tip>
  Make sure the ingredients and instructions are clearly visible in the photo. Good lighting makes a big difference — try taking the photo in a well-lit area, or increase your screen brightness if you're scanning a screenshot.
</Tip>

<Note>
  If your recipe spans more than one page — like a cookbook spread or a long printed recipe — you can upload up to 3 photos at once to capture it all.
</Note>

## Troubleshooting

If the scan came back with missing or incorrect details, you can always edit any field inside Souschef before saving. For more common issues, see [Why a recipe import might fail](/importing/why-import-fails).
