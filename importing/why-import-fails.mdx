Most imports in Souschef work quickly and smoothly — but every now and then, something doesn't go quite as expected. Don't worry! There are usually simple explanations and easy fixes. Below you'll find the most common reasons an import might fail, along with what you can do about each one.

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="The Instagram or TikTok post doesn't include a recipe">
    Souschef extracts recipe details from the post's caption, comments, and spoken audio. If the video is just a beautiful clip with no recipe details anywhere — no ingredient list in the caption, no instructions in the comments, no voiceover narrating the steps — then there's nothing for Souschef to extract.

    **What to try:** Look for a post where the creator has included the recipe in the caption or comments, or spoken through the ingredients and steps in the video. These tend to work best.

  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="The import is taking too long or got stuck">
    Instagram and TikTok imports can take up to 30 seconds because Souschef is analyzing the caption, comments, and audio. If it seems stuck well beyond that, something may have gone wrong in the process.

    **What to try:** Close the Souschef app completely, then reopen it and try the import again. Make sure you have a stable internet connection before retrying.

  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="The recipe website uses an unsupported format">
    Souschef works with most popular recipe blogs, but some websites use custom or non-standard layouts that make it harder to extract structured recipe data.

    **What to try:** If the import is incomplete, you can copy the URL and try importing it again — sometimes a retry does the trick. Alternatively, take a screenshot of the recipe page and use the [Scan feature](/importing/import-from-photos) to extract it from the image instead.

  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="The extracted recipe is missing information">
    AI extraction is smart, but it's not perfect. Sometimes an ingredient quantity, a step, or the recipe title might be missing or slightly off — especially if the original post or page was formatted in an unusual way.

    **What to try:** You can edit any field inside Souschef before saving. Tap any ingredient, step, or title to update it. It only takes a moment to fill in what's missing and you'll end up with a perfect recipe ready to cook.

  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Souschef isn't appearing in my share sheet">
    If you can't find the Souschef icon when you tap Share in Instagram, TikTok, YouTube, or your browser, it may not have been added to your share menu yet.

    **What to try:** Follow our guide on [how to add Souschef to your share menu](/getting-started/add-to-share-menu) — it only takes a minute and you'll only need to do it once.

  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="The import failed with an error message">
    Occasionally a temporary issue can cause an import to fail with an error — this can happen due to a brief network hiccup or a problem on the source platform's end.

    **What to try:** Wait a moment and try the import again. If it keeps failing after a couple of attempts, please [contact our support team](/troubleshooting/contact-support) and let us know which source you were importing from and what the error said — we'll look into it right away.

  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

<Tip>
  You can always edit a recipe after importing — tap the recipe in your
  collection, then tap **Edit** to fix anything that didn't extract correctly.
  You're never stuck with the first result!
</Tip>
