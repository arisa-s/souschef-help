Souschef can save any recipe you spot on Facebook — from a friend's shared post to a viral cooking video or reel. Share the post to Souschef (or paste its URL) and Souschef reads the caption, comments, and any spoken audio to build a full, structured recipe you can cook, scale, and add to your shopping list.

<Note>
  Souschef works best with Facebook posts that include the recipe details in the caption, comments, or spoken audio. Purely visual posts without recipe details may not produce a complete recipe.
</Note>

## Import a Facebook post through the share menu

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the recipe post in the Facebook app">
    Find the post, video, or reel you want to save in the Facebook app or Facebook mobile browser.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Tap Share and choose 'More'">
    Tap the **Share** button on the post, then choose **Share to another app** (or **More**) to open your phone's share sheet.

    <Info>
      📸 Screenshot needed: a Facebook post with the Share menu open and the 'Share to another app' option highlighted.
    </Info>
  </Step>
  <Step title="Select Souschef">
    Tap the **Souschef** icon in your share sheet. Souschef will open and start extracting the recipe automatically.

    <Info>
      📸 Screenshot needed: the iOS/Android share sheet with the Souschef icon highlighted.
    </Info>

    Don't see Souschef in the share sheet? Follow [Add Souschef to your share menu](/getting-started/add-to-share-menu).
  </Step>
  <Step title="Review and save">
    Souschef processes the caption, comments, and audio — this usually takes up to 30 seconds. Review the extracted recipe, edit anything that needs tweaking, and tap **Save**.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Import a Facebook recipe from a URL

If you'd rather work from a link — for example, a post someone sent you in a message:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Copy the Facebook post URL">
    Tap the three dots on the post and choose **Copy link**, or copy the URL from your browser's address bar.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Open Souschef and tap '+'">
    Open Souschef and tap the **\+** button at the bottom of your library.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Paste the URL">
    Choose **Import from URL** and paste the Facebook link. Tap **Import** and let Souschef do its thing.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Facebook recipes in AI Discovery

Facebook is one of the sources [AI Discovery](/using/ai-discovery) searches when you describe what you're in the mood for. If a Facebook post is a strong match for your query, it will appear right alongside results from Instagram, TikTok, YouTube, and recipe websites — and you can import it into your collection with a single tap.

<Tip>
  For reels and video posts, keep Souschef open while it processes the recipe. Audio analysis takes a bit longer than a caption-only extraction, but the results are much richer.
</Tip>

## Troubleshooting

If a Facebook import came back empty or is missing details, see [Why a recipe import might fail](/importing/why-import-fails). You can also edit any recipe after saving — just tap **Edit** on the recipe.
